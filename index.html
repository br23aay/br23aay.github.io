About Me

I am a recent MSc graduate in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics from the University of Hertfordshire, awarded with Commendation (equivalent to Distinction). My MSc project — training a 24-degree-of-freedom Shadow Hand robotic platform to perform dexterous manipulation using Proximal Policy Optimisation (PPO) in MuJoCo — was published in a peer-reviewed journal with an Impact Factor of 7.52.

My work sits at the intersection of reinforcement learning, AI safety and LLM engineering. I have hands-on experience with reward specification problems, RAG pipeline development, and model evaluation — and I am particularly interested in how lessons from robotic RL apply to alignment challenges in large language models.

I hold a valid Graduate Visa (Post-Study Work) with full right to work in the UK — no sponsorship required. I am available immediately.

Published Research

IJRES · Vol. 13, Issue 6 · Impact Factor 7.52 · April 2025
Reinforcement Learning for Robot Dexterous In-Hand Manipulation of Objects (Shadow Hand)
Rachuri, B. & Faria, D.R. · ISSN: 2320-9364 · pp. 164–183 · University of Hertfordshire
Trained a Shadow Hand (24 degrees of freedom) robotic platform to rotate a pen 180° using PPO in a custom MuJoCo simulation environment. Designed a phase-based reward function (grasp → rotate → hold) to address reward hacking behaviours observed in early training runs — a firsthand encounter with the reward misspecification problem central to AI safety research.
